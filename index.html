About Us

Consortium for community development brings together liked minded citizens and community based initiatives to form partnerships on one platform for sharing skills and resources for sustainable development.
Our projects mainly focus on economically and socially empowering women, youth and ensuring that rights of women and children are highly respected.

1. Masese 1 Youth Cage Fish Farmer’s Project.

A team of youths in Masese jinja have abandoned illegal methods of fishing and resulted in to a rather profitable and legal agribusiness activity called cage fish farming.
This involves raising fish commercially in an enclosure known as cages. The youths acquire fish fingerlings for Nile Tilapia at a very tender stage weighing around 0.3-1g and they raise it for a period of 9-10 moths.
They average weight harvested at this stage is 400-500g Fish farming has played a very big role in creating employment for theseread more... youths in the process improving their livelihood and income generation.
The national economic development has been boosted as well as providing nutrition to the surrounding population.
Cage fish farming activity is targeting the growing number of unemployed youth fishermen who are currently unemployed after the government deployed the army to control illegal fishing practices resulting into fish depletion and low fish exports thereby affecting the national economy.
The project beneficiaries have been the unemployed youth who have acquired technical skills and in return have become supervisors, monitors and instructors of cage fish farmers.
The skilled have skilled the unskilled thereby creating an environment of growth and development in this area.

2.Securing Uganda’s rights women and girls.

Social and economically empowering women and girls through advocating for their rights to ownership of productive assets and reduce domestic violence.
We empower women through skills training and providing support to women-led enterprises and providing educational scholarships.
We strongly believe that economically empowered women will have strong ability to bargain for individual rights and also be able to participate in the day to day decision making of society thereby positively reducing gender discrimination.

3. Community led action for children and families

Working with schools, health centers and other community services programs to ensure they provide quality standard service for the benefit of children and families they serve.
We engage in capacity building project e.g. providing equipments for schools and hospitals, building better learning facilities for special needs programs.
Domestic violence/abuse of rights of children and women is highly driven by addiction.
Addiction has been a major catalyst to violence and poverty in families. We therefore work through rehabilitation activities to give support victims of violence and rehabilitate addicts
